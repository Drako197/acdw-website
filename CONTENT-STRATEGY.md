## Executive Summary

AC Drain Wiz's website content strategy focuses on three core pillars:

1. **Problem-Solution Framework**: Address water damage prevention as the primary pain point
2. **Product Education**: Clear hierarchy featuring Mini (flagship), Sensor (smart), and Core 1.0 (proven)
3. **Audience Segmentation**: Tailored experiences for Homeowners, HVAC Professionals, Distributors, and City/Code Officials

**Primary Goals**:
- Drive product awareness and education
- Generate qualified leads across all customer segments
- Position AC Drain Wiz as the trusted leader in AC drain line maintenance
- Increase conversion rates through clear value propositions

## Call-to-Action Strategy

### Primary CTAs (High Intent)

1. **"Buy Now"**
   - Context: Product pages, hero sections
   - Action: Direct to checkout/product page
   - Audience: Ready buyers
   - Design: Bold, primary color

2. **"Schedule Demo"**
   - Context: Professional/distributor sections
   - Action: Contact form with calendar
   - Audience: B2B prospects
   - Design: Secondary color, prominent

3. **"Contact HVAC Pro"**
   - Context: Bundled solutions
   - Action: Professional consultation form
   - Audience: Homeowners seeking installation
   - Design: Secondary, supportive

### Secondary CTAs (Educational)

1. **"Learn More"**
   - Context: Product cards, feature sections
   - Action: Navigate to product details
   - Audience: Researchers
   - Design: Outline style, less prominent

2. **"See How It Works"**
   - Context: Hero section, product features
   - Action: Video/demo content
   - Audience: Visual learners
   - Design: Outline style with icon

3. **"Download Guide"**
   - Context: Educational content
   - Action: PDF download with email capture
   - Audience: Detailed researchers
   - Design: Text link or small button

### Tertiary CTAs (Navigation)

1. **"Explore Solutions"**
   - Context: Customer type cards
   - Action: Navigate to segmented content
   - Audience: All visitors
   - Design: Standard button

2. **"See Specifications"**
   - Context: Product pages
   - Action: Scroll to technical details
   - Audience: Technical researchers
   - Design: Text link

3. **"Find Installer"**
   - Context: DIY vs. professional decision
   - Action: Installer directory/form
   - Audience: Homeowners needing help
   - Design: Secondary button

### CTA Placement Guidelines

**Above the Fold**:
- Primary CTA always visible
- Maximum one secondary CTA
- Clear visual hierarchy

**Within Content**:
- CTAs at logical decision points
- Contextually relevant actions
- Avoid CTA overload (max 2 per section)

**End of Page**:
- Reinforce primary action
- Offer alternative path
- Include contact information

## Content Governance

### Content Ownership

**Content Types**:
1. **Website Copy**: Marketing team
2. **Technical Content**: Product team with marketing review
3. **Blog Posts**: Content team with SEO optimization
4. **Product Descriptions**: Product team with marketing polish

### Review Process

1. **Draft Creation**: Content owner creates initial draft
2. **Technical Review**: Product/engineering team verifies accuracy
3. **Marketing Review**: Marketing team optimizes messaging
4. **Legal Review**: Compliance check for claims and guarantees
5. **Final Approval**: Marketing director approves publication

### Update Schedule

**Regular Updates**:
- Product pages: Quarterly review
- Homepage: Monthly optimization
- Blog content: Weekly new posts
- SEO content: Bi-weekly keyword review

**Event-Driven Updates**:
- New product launches
- Feature releases
- Pricing changes
- Market shifts
- Competitive responses

### Content Performance Metrics

**Engagement Metrics**:
- Page views
- Time on page
- Scroll depth
- Video completion rate
- Click-through rate

**Conversion Metrics**:
- Form submissions
- Product purchases
- Demo requests
- Download completions
- Email signups

**SEO Metrics**:
- Organic traffic
- Keyword rankings
- Featured snippets
- Backlinks
- Domain authority

### Brand Voice Guidelines

**Do's**:
- Use active voice
- Address customer pain points directly
- Emphasize benefits over features
- Include social proof
- Be specific with claims

**Don'ts**:
- Use industry jargon without explanation
- Make unsubstantiated claims
- Overuse superlatives
